The D-parameter was originally introduced by Southworth & Hawkins (1963) for meteor shower identification; it is essentially a measure of the similarity between a pair of orbits denoted as A and B:

\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
    D_{A,B}^2 = (q_B - q_A)^2 + (e_B - e_A)^2 + \left(2\sin{\dfrac{I}{2}}\right)^2
    \\ + \left[(e_A + e_B)\sin{\dfrac{\Pi}{2}}\right]^2 ~,
\end{split}
\end{equation}

where,

\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
    I = \arccos[{\cos{i_A}\cos{i_B}+\sin{i_A}\sin{i_B}\cos{\Omega_A-\Omega_B}}]~,\\
    \Pi = \omega_A - \omega_B + 2\arcsin{\left(\cos{\dfrac{i_A + i_B}{2}\sin{\dfrac{\Omega_A - \Omega_B}{2}\sec{\dfrac{I}{2}}}}{}\right)}~.
\end{split}
\end{equation}
